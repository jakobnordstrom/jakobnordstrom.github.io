Getting to Copenhagen and/or Lund

If you are travelling by air, the Copenhagen Airport Kastrup (CPH) is probably the most convenient option, although Malmö Airport (MMX) in Sturup might also be an alternative.

From Copenhagen Airport you can take the train to either Lund or central Copenhagen, and there is also a Copenhagen metro station at the airport.

From Malmö Airport there are airport buses to Malmö, and then you can take a 10-minute train ride onwards to Lund or cross the bridge to go to Copenhagen.

Directions to the University of Copenhagen

To get from Copenhagen Airport to DIKU, the Department of Computer Science at the University of Copenhagen, which is located at Nørre Campus at Universitetsparken 1, one possibility is to take the train to Nørreport station (one stop after the central station København H) and then follow the directions onwards to DIKU spelled out in text below.

If arriving to Nørreport with the train from København H, exit the train platform in the direction indicated by signs to buses. When coming up the stairs, keep going straight until the last bus stop on the left before you would have to cross a road (200 meters, maybe). This is close to Netto, Forex, and Gothersgade street. From that bus stop, take buses 15E, 150, or 185 to the bus stop Universitetsparken. This takes 5-10 minutes.

When you get off the bus, cross the road to the left and then keep going straight in the direction of travel for a few hundred meters until you see a white sign for Faculty of Science, Department of Computer Science at Nørre Allé 63B, Bygning 771. Turn left there and try to get into the door. Then walk one flight of stairs up, take a right towards BARC, and take a right up the stairs again to the BARC second floor and ring the bell. My office is 3-1-09 (with a MIAO poster on the door). If we are slow to open, please try to text or call Jakob Nordström or some other MIAO group member.

If the door from Nørre Allé is locked, then walk around the building and enter from the main entrance at Universitetsparken instead — in this case you take a right after the stairs immediately after the entrance and then a right again up to the second floor.

If you are close to a metro station then you can also take the metro line M3 to Vibenshus Runddel, from where you walk half a kilometer along Nørre Allé.

You can download the DOT app for public transport tickets in Copenhagen at dinoffentligetransport.dk/en/find-tickets/dot-tickets-app.

Directions to Lund University

To get from Copenhagen Airport to the Department of Computer Science at the Faculty of Engineering (LTH) at Lund University, which is located at Klas Anshelms väg 10 / Ole Römers väg 3, take the Öresund commuter train across the Öresund Bridge to Lund. Please do not forget to bring your passport along, as you will be crossing the border between Sweden and Denmark and there are sometimes passport controls. All commuter trains from Copenhagen to any destination in Sweden will pass through Lund ca 10 minutes after Malmö Central Station.

The easiest way of buying tickets to Sweden might be to download the Skånetrafiken app. You can adjust the settings so that you get e-mailed receipts for all purchases. There are also ticket machines at Copenhagen Airport.

The most convenient route onwards from Lund Central Station would seem to be to take the tram to the "Lund LTH" stop, which takes just a few minutes, and then walk from there a few minutes minutes more. If you are coming with the standard Öresund commuter train from Copenhagen, then the local public transport in Lund is included in your train ticket.

To reach the tram stop, take the stairs at the middle exit of the platform at Lund Central Station (usually at the front of the train in the direction of travel if you are coming from Copenhagen/Malmö, or close to the first train cars), take a right, and then walk straight out of the train station and cross one small and one slightly larger street. Now you should see a bus stop to your left, and some 20-30 meters behind that bus stop there is a tram station.

After getting off the tram at the "Lund LTH" stop, walk right to John Ericssons väg and continue straight, then take a right before you reach the Vattenhallen Science Center to reach the main entrance at Klas Anshelms väg 10. To get to the MIAO research group, bear right after you have entered the building and walk up the stairs one floor up, take a left turn and then immediately a right turn — you should now be in front of a locked door with a sign "Datavetenskap" (i.e., Computer Science). If anyone is there and opens for you, then just continue straight past a few doors until you see offices with MIAO posters on the doors. My office is 2130a. If you cannot get in, try to text or call Jakob Nordström or some other MIAO group member.
