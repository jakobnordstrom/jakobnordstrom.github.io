Supplementary material for Cutting to the Core of Pseudo-Boolean Optimization: Combining Core-Guided Search with Cutting Planes Reasoning

This page contains links to code and benchmarks for the paper

  • Jo Devriendt, Stephan Gocht, Emir Demirovic, Jakob Nordström, and Peter J. Stuckey. Cutting to the Core of Pseudo-Boolean Optimization: Combining Core-Guided Search with Cutting Planes Reasoning In Proceedings of the 35th AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence (AAAI '21), 2021.

Code and Experimental Data

Code and experimental data for the paper can be found in the Zenodo repository doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.4043124.

Benchmarks

  • The OPT-SMALL-INT benchmarks from the PB16 Competition can be found at www.cril.univ-artois.fr/PB16/.
  • The MIPLIB instances we used are collected at zenodo.org/record/3870965. We converted a collection of 0-1 ILP instances from the benchmark sets MIPLIB 2, 3, 2003, 2010 and 2017 to OPB. Some of the instances were rescaled to make all integers small enough for the (then fixed-precion version of the) RoundingSat solver.
  • Our KNAPSACK instances are from
    • Pisinger, D. Where Are the Hard Knapsack Problems? Computers & Operations Research 32(9), 2271-2284 (2005).
    The paper is available at doi.org/10.1016/j.cor.2004.03.002), and the benchmarks converted to OPB can be found at zenodo.org/record/3939055.
  • Some crafted combinatorial benchmarks inspired by proof complexity, referred to as CRAFT in the paper, can be found below:
    • composed_pebbling_php_opt_pyramid
    • comppebphp_pyrheight_equals_nholes
    • comppebvc
    • dominating_set
    • evencolouring
    • injcomp
    • linpeb
    • maxcut
    • multverif
    • vertex_cover
